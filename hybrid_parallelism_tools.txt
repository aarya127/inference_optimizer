Why it stands out
Multimodal models (Llama 3.2, Qwen-VL, Pixtral) are exploding in use.

Serving them efficiently is unsolved and painful today.

Vision encoders are often too small for tensor parallelism, but frameworks still apply TP blindly.

A system that automatically chooses TP vs DP based on encoder size, resolution, and GPU topology would be immediately useful to:

vLLM

TensorRT-LLM

Triton

Any enterprise deploying VLMs

Why people would care
Companies are actively struggling with multimodal inference scaling.

A 30–45% throughput improvement is enormous in production.

This is publishable as systems research and shippable as an open-source tool.

Why it’s novel
No serving framework today does dynamic parallelism selection.

No one has published a systematic study of “when TP hurts more than it helps” for VLMs.

This is the kind of project that gets attention from:

ML systems researchers

GPU engineers

Industry practitioners

Open-source maintainers
